Green Tea

Green tea is great for you and has undeniable benefits for your body and mind. But are you sure you’re making it the right way? If your green tea ends up tasting grassy or bitter, then hold up...you’re making it wrong and you might be losing out on vital nutrients too.




Preparing Green Tea With Leaves

Ingredients:

  • Green tea leaves – 1 teaspoon for 1 cup of green tea. You can use green tea pearls.
  • A clean tea strainer
  • A glass teapot/stainless steel pan
  • A cup of water




Method:

  1. Take the appropriate amount of green tea leaves based on the number of cups you want to make.
  2. Place the tea leaves inside a sieve or strainer.
  3. Heat water in a pot to a temperature of 80-85°C.
  4. Place the strainer or sieve over the cup.
  5. Pour the hot water into the cup, letting the green tea steep for 2-3 minutes. Taste the tea every 30-45 seconds to adjust to your preferred flavor.
  6. Set the sieve aside. Optionally, you can add a spoon of honey or a sprinkle of cinnamon to sweeten the tea. Allow the tea to cool slightly before adding honey.
  7. Enjoy your fresh cup of green tea!



Preparing Green Tea With Leaves

Ingredients:

  • Stainless steel pot
  • Cup of hot water
  • A green tea bag of good quality
  • A clay cup/stainless steel cup
  • A lid to cover the cup




Method:

  1. Place the green tea bag into the cup.
  2. Heat the water at 80-85 degrees Celsius and pour into the cup.
  3. Cover the cup for 3 minutes.
  4. Remove the lid and the tea bag, and stir the tea.
  5. Have a sip. The tea is ready!
